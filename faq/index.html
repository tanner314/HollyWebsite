What is a typical lesson like with you?

Prior to the lesson, students will complete a 5 minute pre-recorded warm-up that will be sent via email after you decide to book with me. Upon arrival, I like to have a warm cup of tea—or apple cider—prepared for my students to sip on throughout their lesson. The vocal coach I worked with in high school did this for me and it was the best. Then, we will discuss goals and songs that we will work on. I do not play piano, so the instrumental track will be played through a speaker. If I hear something vocally that needs to be adjusted, I may stop the music midway through so we can work on it. After the lesson ends, I let my students pick a candy or a prize.

What styles of music do you specialize in?

I specialize in pop, electronic, and the Great American Songbook. However, I can work with most styles.

How long until improvement?

I give my students the tools to improve during our first lesson. Long term improvement can be expected after a minimum of 6 months.

What can a student do to improve their voice outside of lessons?

Hydrate. Hydrate. Hydrate. Drinking water throughout the day is so important for the health of our vocal cords. Also, students should practice the pre-recorded warm-ups (5 minutes) and songs (15-20 minutes) every other day if possible.

What if a student is late to a lesson or forgets?

Life happens, so I extend a 10-minute grace period. If a student cannot make their lesson, please let me know by 12pm the day of the lesson so we can schedule a make-up lesson. If a student is a no-show and no notice is given, the full lesson amount will be charged and no make-up lesson will be offered.

Do you offer any discounts?

I offer $20 off the monthly lesson fee for healthcare workers, military, and first responders. Thank you for all that you do.
